    Recursive?

    Now, you should know waht is resursive. So we will present some mathmatics for the recursion.

    Master Theorem

    Definition

    The recurrence relation is :
    $$ T(n) = aT({n \over b}) + f(n) , a \geq 1, b > 1$$

    • case 1: if $ f(n)$ is equal to ${ O(n^{log_b{a}-\epsilon}) }$
      • then ${T(n) = O(n^{log_b{a}})}$
    • case 2: if f(n) is equal to ${\Theta(n^{log_b{a}}log^{k}(n)), k \geq 0}$
      • then ${T(n) = \theta(n^{log_b{a}}log^{k+1}(n))}$, $ \exists k \geq 0 $
      • also ${T(n) = O(n^{log_b{a}}log(n))}$
    • case 3: if f(n) is equal to ${\Omega(n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon})}$
      • then ${T(n) = O(f(n))}$
    • case 4: if f(n) is equal to ${\Theta(n^{log_b{a}}log^{k}(n)), k = -1}$
      • then ${T(n) = \theta(n^{log_b{a}}loglog(n))}$

    Proof

    First, the recursive tree will give you the overview of master theorem.

    • Tree hight: ${log_b{n}}$
    • the element of each level: ${a^{level}}$
    • the number of leaf: ${a^{log_b{n}}}$
    Total cost
    • Total cast = all leaves + all node weight
      • all leaves = ${ \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) }$
      • all node weight = ${ {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}{f({n \over {b^i}})}}$

    Second, we will define each case with summation of each node weight by the following function:
    $$ w(n) = {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}{f({n \over {b^i}})} $$

    1. case 1: substitute ${ O(n^{log_b{a}-\epsilon}) }$ into ${f({n \over {b^i}})}$
      • ${ {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}{({n \over {b^i}})^{log_b{a}-\epsilon} }}$
      • the n is not related with the summation parameter, so
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}-\epsilon}}{\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}({1 \over {b^i}})^{log_b{a}-\epsilon}}$
      • ${b^{(log_b{a})-(\epsilon)}}$ is equal ${ {b^{(log_b{a})}}\over{b^{\epsilon}} }$
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}-\epsilon}} {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}({ab^{\epsilon} \over {b^{log_b{a}}}})^{i} }$
      • the ${ b^{log_b{a}} }$ is equal ${ a^{log_b{b}}} $
      • use geometric series formulas
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}-\epsilon}}({n^{\epsilon} - 1 \over {b^{\epsilon} - 1}}) }$
      • since b and $\epsilon$ are constants then ${ {n^{log_b{a}-\epsilon}}O(n^\epsilon) }$
      • Total cast:
        • ${ \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) + w(n) = \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) + O(n^{log_b{a}})}$
        • ${O(n^{log_b{a}})}$
    2. case 2: substitute ${ \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}{log^{k}(n)}) }$ into ${f({n \over {b^i}})}$
      • ${ {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}{({n \over {b^i}})^{log_b{a}} }log^{k}(n)}$
      • the n is not related with the summation parameter, so
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}}}log^{k}(n){\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}({1 \over {b^i}})^{log_b{a}}}$
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}}}log^{k}(n) {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}({a \over {b^{log_b{a}}}})^{i} }$
      • the ${ b^{log_b{a}} }$ is equal ${ a^{log_b{b}}} $
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}}}log^{k}(n) {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}({a \over a})^{i} }$
      • use arithmetic series formulas
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}}}log^{k}(n)({log_b(n)+1}) }$
      • Changing the base
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}}}log^{k}(n)({log(n) \over log(b)}+1) }$
      • ${ (c1){n^{log_b{a}}}{log^{k+1}(n)} \leq{n^{log_b{a}}}log^{k}(n)({log(n) \over log(b)}+1) \leq (c2){n^{log_b{a}}}{log^{k+1}(n)}, \exists c1, c2 \geq 0 }$
      • ${ \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}{log^{k+1}(n))} }$
      • Total cast:
        • ${ \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) + w(n) = \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) + \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}{log^{k+1}(n))}}$
        • ${\Theta(n^{log_b{a}}{log^{k+1}(n))}}$
    3. case 3: substitute ${ \Omega(n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}) }$ into ${f({n \over {b^i}})}$
      • ${ {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}{({n \over {b^i}})^{log_b{a}+\epsilon} }}$
      • the n is not related with the summation parameter, so
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}{\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}{a^i}({1 \over {b^i}})^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}$
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}} {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}({a \over {b^{log_b{a} + \epsilon}}})^{i} }$
      • the ${ b^{log_b{a}} }$ is equal ${ a^{log_b{b}}} $
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}} {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}({a \over {ab^\epsilon}})^{i} }$
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}} {\sum}_{i=0}^{log_b{n-1}}({1 \over {b^\epsilon}})^{i} }$
      • use geometric series formulas
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}({b^{-\epsilon{log_b{n}}} - 1 \over {b^{-\epsilon} - 1}}) }$
      • ${ {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}({n^{-\epsilon} - 1 \over {b^{-\epsilon} - 1}}) \leq {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}({1 \over {1-b^{-\epsilon}}}) }$, all sufficiently large $n$
      • $ \because a({n \over b})^{log_b{a}+\epsilon} \leq c{n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}$
        • $ {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}({a \over {ab^\epsilon}}) \leq c{n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}} }$
        • $ {b^{-\epsilon} \leq c , \exists c < 1}$
      • $\therefore {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}({1 \over {1-b^{-\epsilon}}}) \leq {n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}}({1 \over {1-c}})$, c is constant
      • $O(n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}) = O(f(n))$
      • Total cast:
        • ${ \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) + w(n) = \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) + O(f(n))}$
        • $\because f(n)=\Omega(n^{log_b{a}+\epsilon}) \geq \Theta(n^{log_b{a}}) $
        • $\therefore$ Tatal cost: $O(f(n)) $

    Reference

    Cormen, Thomas H.; Leiserson, Charles E.; Rivest, Ronald L.; Stein, Clifford (2009) [1990]. Introduction to Algorithms (3rd ed.). MIT Press and McGraw-Hill.
